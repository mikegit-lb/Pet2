Reading Part 3

Questions 11–15
Read the article and choose the correct answer.

My First Part-Time Job

When I started working at a small café on Saturdays, I expected the job to be easy. I imagined making coffee, taking money and chatting to customers. On my first day, however, I discovered that the work was much busier than I had thought.

The manager, Paula, showed me how to use the machine and explained the menu. I made several mistakes at first. I forgot one customer’s sandwich and gave another customer the wrong drink. Paula stayed calm and told me that everyone needs time to learn.

After a few weeks, I became faster and more confident. I also learned how important it is to listen carefully. Some customers were in a hurry, while others wanted to talk. I had to understand what each person needed.

The best part of the job is not the money, although that is useful. I enjoy feeling responsible and being part of a team. I now think every teenager should try a part-time job because it teaches things that school cannot always teach.

Explanations & Improvement Advice

Before the real PET exam

Reading explanations

Listening advice

Main skill: Listen for the final decision, not every word you hear. PET often includes distractors such as “I wanted pasta, but I chose soup.”

Areas to improve

Reading: Build skill with paraphrasing, synonyms, linkers, pronoun reference and gap logic.
Writing: Answer every bullet point, use paragraphs, and check B1 tenses carefully.
Listening: Practise changed plans, numbers, dates, locations and similar-sounding options.
Speaking: Extend every answer with a reason or example. Use “I agree because…”, “What do you think?”, and “Shall we choose…?” in Part 3.
